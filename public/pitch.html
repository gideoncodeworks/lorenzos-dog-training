Issue #1

Missing Meta Descriptions

ü§î What is a meta description?

It's the short summary that appears under your website name in Google search results. It's like a mini-advertisement that tells people what your page is about and why they should click.

‚ö†Ô∏è Why does this matter?

Without meta descriptions, Google just grabs random text from your page. It often looks messy and confusing. People see a jumbled preview and skip to a competitor whose description clearly says "Professional dog training in Cleveland - Call today!"

üî¥ Your Current Status

Zero pages on your website have meta descriptions. Every single page is showing random, confusing text in search results.

Issue #2

Multiple H1 Tags (Confusing Headlines)

ü§î What is an H1 tag?

It's the main headline of your page ‚Äî like the title of a chapter in a book. It tells Google "This is what this page is about." Every page should have exactly ONE main topic.

‚ö†Ô∏è Why does this matter?

When you have multiple H1s, it's like having a book chapter with 5 different titles. Google gets confused: "Is this page about puppy training? Service dogs? Obedience? Locations?" When Google is confused, it doesn't rank you.

üî¥ Your Current Status

Your pages have multiple H1 tags, sending mixed signals to Google about what each page is actually about.

Issue #3

All Images Missing Alt Text

ü§î What is alt text?

Alt text is a description you add to images that tells Google what the picture shows. Google can't "see" images ‚Äî it can only read text. So without alt text, all your photos of dogs, training sessions, and facilities are invisible to Google.

‚ö†Ô∏è Why does this matter?

You probably have dozens of photos showing happy dogs, professional training, beautiful facilities. Each of those could help you rank in Google Image search and regular search. Instead, Google sees... nothing. It's like having billboards that are completely blank.

üî¥ Your Current Status

100% of your images are missing alt text. You're losing massive SEO value from every single photo on your site.

Major Discovery

You Have a Ghost Website

ü§î What do I mean by "ghost website"?

Your OLD website (built on Joomla) is still being indexed by Google. So there are two versions of your business floating around the internet ‚Äî your old site and your current Squarespace site.

‚ö†Ô∏è Why is this a problem?

Google sees both sites and gets confused. It doesn't know which one is "real." So instead of giving you full credit, it splits your ranking power between two sites. It's like having two half-strength versions of yourself competing against full-strength competitors.
